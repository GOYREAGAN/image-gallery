Manchester City Football Club, based in Manchester, England, was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's Church football team.
It became Ardwick Association Football Club in 1887 and adopted the name Manchester City in 1894.
The club's early years were marked by fluctuating success, including its first major trophy, the FA Cup, in 1904

Over the decades, Manchester City experienced highs and lows, including relegations and promotions.
Notable achievements include winning the Football League First Division championship in 1937 and 1968,
as well as the European Cup Winners' Cup in 1970.
The club's fortunes transformed significantly after Sheikh Mansour's acquisition in 2008,
leading to consistent top-tier success and multiple Premier League titles.

Manchester City is now recognized as one of the most successful football clubs in England,
with a passionate fan base and a history of resilience. Are you a fan?
